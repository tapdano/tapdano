About TapDano & Seed Vault

TapDano provides a secure solution for managing cryptocurrency wallet seed phrases using NFC (Near Field Communication) technology. The Seed Vault feature allows you to store encrypted wallet seed phrases on physical NFC tags for convenient and secure access.

Key Security Principle: Your seed phrases are never transmitted to or stored on our servers. All encryption and decryption operations occur locally on your device.

How Your Information Is Handled

Data Stored Locally on Your Device

  • NFC Tag Physical ID: Used to verify you're scanning the correct tag
  • Tag Index: Identifies which tag you're currently using
  • Wallet Metadata: Wallet names, types, and encrypted seed phrases
  • App Settings: Your preferences and configuration

Data Stored on NFC Tags

  • Tag ID: A unique identifier linking the tag to your account
  • Encrypted Wallet Data: Your wallet seed phrases encrypted with AES-256 encryption
  • Wallet Metadata: Names and types of wallets stored on the tag

Important: The data on NFC tags is encrypted using a key that only our backend can provide after successful authentication. Without this encryption key, the data on the tag cannot be decrypted.

Data Stored on Our Backend Servers

Our backend stores only the following information:

  • Tag ID: A unique identifier for each NFC tag
  • Physical Tag ID: The hardware identifier of your NFC tag
  • Email Address: For authentication and account recovery via email OTP
  • PIN Hash: A cryptographic hash of your PIN (not the PIN itself)
  • Encryption Key: The key required to decrypt wallet data stored on your NFC tag
  • Device Metadata: Platform, device model, app version, and OS version for security and diagnostics

Critical Privacy Guarantee:

  • Your seed phrases are NEVER sent to our servers
  • Seed phrases remain encrypted on NFC tags and your device
  • We cannot access your seed phrases at any time
  • All wallet generation and seed phrase operations happen entirely on your device

How Seed Vault Works

1. Tag Setup & Registration

When you set up a new NFC tag:

  1. The App generates a unique Tag ID
  2. You create a PIN and provide an email address
  3. The App generates an encryption key locally
  4. The encryption key, PIN hash, and email are sent to our backend
  5. The Tag ID is written to your NFC tag

2. Adding Wallets

When you create or import a wallet:

  1. You generate a new seed phrase on your device or import an existing one
  2. The seed phrase is encrypted locally on your device using AES-256 encryption
  3. The encrypted data is written to your NFC tag
  4. No seed phrase data is transmitted to our servers

3. Authentication & Access

When you want to access your wallets:

  1. You scan your NFC tag to read the Tag ID and encrypted wallet data
  2. You authenticate using PIN or email OTP
  3. Upon successful authentication, our backend returns the encryption key
  4. The App uses the encryption key to decrypt your wallet data locally on your device
  5. You can now view and use your seed phrases

Backend's Role: Our backend serves as an authentication gateway and secure key vault. It verifies your identity and provides the encryption key needed to decrypt your locally-stored data. It never processes, stores, or has access to your actual seed phrases.

Security Architecture Summary

Data Type Storage Location Encryption
Seed Phrases NFC Tag + Device AES-256 (Local)
Encryption Key Backend Server At Rest (Server)
Email & PIN Hash Backend Server At Rest (Server)
Tag IDs NFC Tag + Backend Not Encrypted

Your Responsibilities

Secure Your Assets

  • Physical Security: Keep your NFC tags in a safe place. Anyone with physical access to your tag can read the encrypted data.
  • Strong PIN: Use a strong, unique PIN. Your PIN is the primary authentication method.
  • Email Security: Secure your email account. It's used for authentication and recovery.
  • Device Security: Keep your mobile device secure with a passcode, biometric lock, and up-to-date software.
  • Backup Strategy: Consider maintaining additional secure backups of your seed phrases outside the App.

Understand the Risks

  • Lost Tags: If you lose your NFC tag and don't have backups, you may lose access to your wallets.
  • Forgotten Credentials: If you forget both your PIN and lose access to your email, you cannot recover your encryption key.
  • Compromised Backend: If our backend is compromised, attackers could potentially obtain encryption keys. However, they would still need physical access to your NFC tag to decrypt your seed phrases.
  • Physical Tag Access: If someone gains both physical access to your tag and your authentication credentials, they can access your wallets.
